# April Fool’s Dinner

## Raison d’être

A tasting menu designed to systematically dismantle diner expectations. Each course targets a different cognitive shortcut (texture, flavor, cultural context, temperature, structure), culminating in a deliberate “control” course that is completely honest (but only after setting the expectation that nothing is normal). The final courses then reintroduce deception after trust has been temporarily restored.

The goal is not just surprise, but **progressive loss of interpretive confidence** — diners stop trusting not only the food, but their ability to understand what they are eating.

---

## Design Principles

- Each course breaks a _different_ assumption (no repeated tricks)
- Escalation from sensory deception → conceptual deception → psychological deception
- One **fully honest course** (entrée) to reset trust
- Final courses exploit that regained trust
- Plating and service are part of the deception

## Menu

Compressed watermelon diced like raw tuna, topped with black garlic dashi caviar and chiffonade basil  
Savory preparation of fruit masquerading as fish tartar — the identity deception starts immediately

### Salad — _Strawberries & Cream_

Strawberries with burrata or stracciatella, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper  
Looks like dessert, eats like a caprese-style salad — expectation flips at first bite

Strawberries & “Cream” (Savory Illusion) — 5 servings

Yield

5 servings (8 oz coupe each)

Ingredients

Roasted Strawberries
• 750 g strawberries, hulled, halved (slightly underripe preferred)
• 2½ tsp olive oil
• 2½ tsp white balsamic vinegar (or very light aged balsamic)
• Pinch kosher salt

Blitzed “Cream” (Stracciatella Base)
• 500 g stracciatella
• 50–75 g heavy cream (3–5 tbsp)
• 50 g mascarpone (optional, for stability)
• Pinch fine salt

Pistachio Crumb
• 75 g shelled pistachios (unsalted)
• 2½ tsp olive oil
• Pinch salt

Final Assembly
• High-quality extra virgin olive oil (peppery)
• Flaky salt
• Coarse black pepper

Preparation 1 — Roasted Strawberries 1. Heat oven to 400°F / 205°C. 2. Toss strawberries with olive oil, vinegar, and salt. 3. Arrange cut-side down on parchment-lined trays (use two if crowded). 4. Roast 8–12 minutes, until:
• Just softened
• Glossy
• Still holding shape (not jammy) 5. Cool to room temperature.

Preparation 2 — Blitzed “Cream” 1. Drain stracciatella in a sieve 5–10 minutes. 2. Transfer to a tall container. 3. Add heavy cream and salt (and mascarpone if using). 4. Blitz in short pulses (total 5–10 sec) until smooth. 5. Whisk lightly to finish.

Target: smooth, glossy, holds shape briefly without pooling.

Preparation 3 — Pistachio Crumb 1. Chop/pulse pistachios to a coarse crumb. 2. Toss with olive oil and salt. 3. Toast in a dry pan over medium heat 2–3 minutes until fragrant. 4. Cool completely.

Final Assembly (per serving) 1. Add ~100 g cream to bottom of coupe; smooth or form a low quenelle. 2. Top with ~150 g roasted strawberries, arranged cleanly. 3. Sprinkle ~1 tbsp pistachio crumb. 4. Drizzle olive oil lightly over fruit. 5. Finish with:
• Flaky salt
• Coarse black pepper

Service Notes
• Serve cool (not cold)
• Keep glass rims clean
• Build just before serving for best texture contrast

### Appetizer — _Bastiolo_

Hybrid of Moroccan bastilla and Italian raviolo  
Crisp pastry enclosing a spiced, savory-sweet filling; familiar form with displaced cuisine identity

---

### Secret Intermezzo — _Ice Cream Stroganoff_

Savory frozen cream base with mushroom, onion, and umami components  
Cold temperature applied to a dish universally expected to be hot

---

### Entrée — _Chicken Pot Pie_

Completely traditional preparation, served from a baking dish  
No deception — the “control” course that induces doubt by its normalcy

---

### Dessert — _Tres Maíces_

White chocolate corn cremoux topped with candy corn crumble and blackened corn husk powder  
Three forms of corn — the vegetable in the cremoux, the candy in the crumble, and the burnt husks as powder — a study in transformation and the suspension of disbelief

---

### Mignardise — _Violently Clarified Consommé_

Crystal clear chicken consommé served in cordial glasses  
After an evening of elaborate deception, this is pure, honest, transparent — made using the "violence plus raw chicken" clarification method from the pot pie stock

---

## Narrative Arc

1. **Immediate identity deception** (watermelon tartar — fruit as fish)
2. **Flavor contradiction** (dessert-looking salad)
3. **Cultural displacement** (bastiolo)
4. **Sensory disruption** (cold savory stroganoff)
5. **Trust reset** (completely normal pot pie)
6. **Ingredient transfiguration** (tres maíces — corn as candy, candy as powder, husk as ash)
7. **Radical honesty** (crystal clear consommé — transparency as the final trick)

---

## Outcome

A dinner where the final state is not “that was surprising,” but:

> “I don’t trust what I’m eating anymore — even when it’s real.”
